Reflection

The Future.

Since the first time I programmed a computer to say “Hello World”, to passing computer science 2050 (my latest achievement in my programming career), I have learned so much about how computers communicate and the specifics of programming. My favorite memory so far in the computer science industry is when me and the guys in my class/grade decided we wanted to make a project over winter break. When we finally got to it, we got in a call on discord, and instead of working on a project, we “worked” on the project by playing games together all night.

My current goals for myself in the industry is to graduate, get my degree, and get some internship/job opportunity in the industry under my belt as well. My ambitions for the future is to find a good job as a software developer, where I actually get to build and implement new features.

Looking Forward.

Career ambitions are often overwhelming and heavy to think about, but I also love sales and communications more than anything. I think in the future, I would love to build and maintain code, but I also wouldn't be opposed to becoming a sales engineer or a task manager. I've heard about the product manager role as well and that seems pretty interesting to me.

Overall, I'm excited to step into the industry and start working. Afterall, that's what these next couple years of hard work is all building up to. I would like to relocate to Seattle after graduation. Sure, the tech industry is thriving up there, almost like a second silicone valley, but it's really the nature and setting of the scenery of the pacific northwest that really gets me. I'm so excited to see what the future will hold for me!
